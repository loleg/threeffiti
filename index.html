This project aims to quantify how graffiti imposes itself on the urban environment, an attempt to study underground culture using open data. With analytics and computer graphics we can juxtapose neighbourhood characteristics with diverse street styles. Public administrations & business in our cities wage a constant, expensive battle to protect buildings from unwanted manifestations. However, it is difficult to get a good high level view of what is going on: there are few statistics and studies, or if they exist they are not open to the public. This experiment is an Urban Data Challenge prototype, that with time could become a virtual gallery, a multifaceted data visualization, a shared online experience.
